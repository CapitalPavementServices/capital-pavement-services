About Asphalt Resurfacing

When a parking lot or private roadway shows widespread surface deterioration — extensive cracking, raveling, oxidation, or rough texture — but the underlying base is still structurally sound, asphalt resurfacing is the smart, cost-effective solution. Also called a mill-and-overlay or thin asphalt overlay, resurfacing delivers a like-new surface at 30 to 50 percent less than full pavement replacement. Capital Pavement Services provides professional asphalt resurfacing services for commercial parking lots and private roadways throughout Northern Virginia.

Our resurfacing process typically involves milling (mechanically grinding) the existing surface to a uniform depth, which removes deteriorated material and creates a clean bond surface for the new asphalt. We address any remaining base issues, make drainage corrections, apply a tack coat to ensure adhesion, and install a new layer of commercial hot-mix asphalt. The finished surface is properly compacted and transitions smoothly to existing pavement, curbs, and drains. After resurfacing, we coordinate restriping to return the lot to full operation.

What’s Included

  • Pavement condition assessment and resurfacing recommendation
  • Surface milling to uniform depth
  • Base repair and drainage corrections where needed
  • Tack coat application for bond between layers
  • Commercial hot-mix asphalt overlay installation
  • Proper compaction and finishing
  • Smooth transitions to curbs, drains, and existing pavement
  • Post-resurfacing restriping coordination
  • Final inspection and walkthrough

Key Benefits

Why Capital Pavement Services

The Right Choice for Asphalt Resurfacing in Northern Virginia

Complete Assessment First

Before recommending resurfacing, we assess whether your base is truly sound enough to support an overlay. Resurfacing over a failing base wastes money. We give you an honest evaluation and recommend the right solution.

Proper Milling & Adhesion

We mill the existing surface and apply tack coat before paving — two steps often skipped by less thorough contractors. These steps are essential for the new overlay to bond properly and deliver the full expected lifespan.

Restriping Included in Planning

Resurfacing covers all existing markings — restriping is essential to return your lot to full use. We coordinate the restriping scope and scheduling as part of every resurfacing project, so there’s no gap between paving and your lot reopening fully.
