About Pavement Crack Filling & Sealing

Cracks in asphalt pavement are far more serious than they appear. Each crack is an entry point for water — and in Northern Virginia, where freeze-thaw cycles occur repeatedly through winter and early spring, water that enters a crack expands when it freezes, widening the crack and pushing apart the pavement. Over time, this process undermines the base, creates potholes, and triggers the kind of widespread structural failure that requires full-depth repair or resurfacing. Capital Pavement Services provides professional pavement crack filling and sealing services throughout Northern Virginia to stop this damage cycle before it becomes expensive.

We use hot-pour rubberized crack filler — the commercial standard for pavement crack sealing. Unlike rigid fillers that crack and pop out during temperature swings, rubberized hot-pour material remains flexible, contracting and expanding with the pavement through Northern Virginia’s seasonal extremes. Our process includes routing wider cracks for better adhesion, cleaning cracks with compressed air, and applying the hot-pour filler in a controlled manner for a flush, durable repair. Properly treated cracks dramatically extend pavement life and significantly reduce the risk of pothole formation.

What’s Included

  • Pavement crack assessment and severity classification
  • Crack routing for transverse and longitudinal cracks
  • Compressed air cleaning of all cracks
  • Hot-pour rubberized crack filler application
  • Overfill blotted with sand for flush, non-tracking finish
  • Treatment of transverse, longitudinal, edge, and block cracks
  • Pre-sealcoating crack treatment
  • Documentation of treated areas
  • Recommendations for cracks requiring deeper repair

Hot-Pour Rubberized Filler

We use commercial hot-pour rubberized crack filler, not cold-pour materials from a hardware store. The rubberized formulation remains flexible through temperature extremes, dramatically outlasting rigid alternatives.

Proper Crack Preparation

We route and clean cracks before filling — a critical step many skip. Routing creates the proper profile for maximum adhesion. Compressed air cleaning removes debris that would prevent the filler from bonding.

Proactive Pavement Protection

Crack filling is the most cost-effective pavement maintenance investment you can make. Treating cracks early prevents the far more expensive consequences — potholes, base failures, and full resurfacing. We help you stay ahead of deterioration.
